Day 1: Let There Be Light

From formless void to divine illumination - the beginning of all creation.

Understanding Day 1

On the very first day, God began His creative work by bringing light into existence where there was only darkness and void. This wasn't the light of the sun (created later), but the fundamental element of light itself - revealing God's power to create ex nihilo (out of nothing).

  • God brings order out of formlessness
  • Light is fundamental, created before the sun
  • God declares the light "good" - establishing His standard
  • The concept of "evening and morning" establishes the 24-hour day cycle from the start

Genesis 1:1-5 (NKJV)

1In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth. 2The earth was without form, and void; and darkness was on the face of the deep. And the Spirit of God was hovering over the face of the waters.

3Then God said, "Let there be light"; and there was light. 4And God saw the light, that it was good; and God divided the light from the darkness. 5God called the light Day, and the darkness He called Night. So the evening and the morning were the first day.
